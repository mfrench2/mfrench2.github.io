Meet Mrs. Zupko

I teach all levels of the Graphic Design and Film Production pathways under the Arts, A/V Technology, and Communications group. My courses help students turn creativity into career-ready skills through hands-on learning, real-world tools, and professional standards. I also teach Dramatic Writing, an embedded English course where students earn credit for both Film Production Applications and ELA. In addition, I serve as the SkillsUSA advisor, supporting student leadership and technical excellence through competitions. Before teaching, I worked in media, design, and marketing. I hold an AAS in Visual Arts, a BS in Marketing and Communication, a Master’s in Executive Leadership, and a second Master’s in Education and Communication. I’ve been married for over 23 years and have two college-aged kids—one recently graduated with a Biology degree from ABAC, and one is studying Neuroscience and Linguistics at Georgia Tech. My husband is a veteran and currently works in law enforcement, and we’ve spent over two decades raising our family with a shared commitment to service and resilience. We share our home with our two pugs. I enjoy collecting rocks and fossils, observing design trends, and thinking about how creativity shapes the way we understand and communicate with the world.

Arts, Audio/Video Technology, and Communications

Graphic Design

  • Introduction to Graphics and Design
  • Graphic Design and Production
  • Advanced Graphic Design

The Graphic Design pathway introduces students to visual communication through hands-on projects using industry-standard tools like Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, and InDesign. Students learn about branding, layout, typography, digital illustration, and production design while developing real-world skills in creative problem-solving and client-based design. This pathway prepares students for careers in design, marketing, and media, with opportunities to earn certifications and create a professional portfolio.

Audio-Video Technology and Film I

  • Audio-Video Technology and Film I
  • Intoduction to Film Production
  • Film Production Applications
  • Audio-Video Technology and Film III

The Film Production pathway teaches storytelling through video, blending creativity with technical skill in areas like screenwriting, directing, cinematography, sound, and editing. Students use industry-standard cameras, lighting, and software to plan, shoot, and produce high-quality media projects. Emphasis is placed on collaboration, visual communication, and learning how to meet professional standards in film and digital content creation.
